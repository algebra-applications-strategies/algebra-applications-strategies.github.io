Example 1.6.50.

Factor \(x^2-12x+35\text{.}\)
🔗
Answer 1.
We are looking to fill in: The two numbers must multiply to \(35\) and add to \(-12\text{.}\) We list the pairs of numbers whose product is \(35\text{,}\) but with the following twist. Note that the sum of the two numbers needs to be negative. So each number must be negative. (It still works to have each pair of numbers multiplying to positive \(35\text{,}\) since a negative times a negative is a positive.)
  • \(-1\) and \(-35\)
    🔗
    🔗
  • \(-5\) and \(-7\)
    🔗
    🔗
Then, look through the list to see which pair of numbers adds up to \(-12\text{.}\) These are \(-5\) and \(-7\text{,}\) so we can fill in the diamond figure as follows: Thus \(x^2-12x+35= (x-5)(x-7)\)
🔗
🔗
Answer 2.
We are looking to fill in the two empty sides in: We consider the product of the first term and last term, \(35 \cdot x^2\text{.}\) Let’s list all pairs of factors of \(35x^2\) where each factor contains an \(x\text{,}\) and each factor is written with a minus sign, because we’ll need the sum to be \(-12x\text{.}\)
  • \(-1x\) and \(-35x\)
    🔗
    🔗
  • \(-5x\) and \(-7x\)
    🔗
    🔗
Then, look through the list to find the pair adds up to \(-12x\text{.}\) These are \(-5x\) and \(-7x\text{,}\) so we can fill in the area model figure as follows: We filled in the areas \(-5x\) and \(-7x\) first. Then the sides \(-5\) and \(-7\) were filled in. Thus \(x^2-12x+35= (x-5)(x-7)\text{.}\)
