Principle 2.4.31. Completing the square when the quadratic coefficient is \(1\).

When the quadratic coefficient is \(1\) (so either a \(1\) is written in front of \(x^2\text{,}\) or nothing is written in front of \(x^2\text{,}\) which siginfies a hidden \(1\)) to complete the square:
  1. Identify the linear coefficient (the number in front of \(x\text{,}\) excluding the \(x\) itself).
    🔗
    🔗
  2. Divide the linear coefficient by \(2\) to get the second stick. (This is technically the "length of the second stick", but we will informally say "second stick".)
    🔗
    🔗
  3. Square the second stick to get the constant that must be added to complete the square.
    🔗
    🔗
