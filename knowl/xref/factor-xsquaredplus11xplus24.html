Example 1.6.44.

Factor \(x^2+11x+24\text{.}\)
🔗
Answer 1.
We are looking to fill in: The two numbers must multiply to \(24\) and add to \(11\text{.}\) We list the pairs of numbers whose product is \(24\text{:}\)
  • \(1\) and \(24\)
    🔗
    🔗
  • \(2\) and \(12\)
    🔗
    🔗
  • \(3\) and \(8\)
    🔗
    🔗
  • \(4\) and \(6\)
    🔗
    🔗
Then, look through the list to see which pair of numbers adds up to \(11\text{.}\) These are \(3\) and \(8\text{,}\) so we can fill in the diamond figure as follows: Thus \(x^2+11x+24= (x+3)(x+8)\)

Answer 2.
We are looking to fill in the two empty sides in: We consider the product of the first term and last term, \(24 \cdot x^2\text{.}\) Let’s list all pairs of factors of \(24x^2\) where each factor contains an \(x\text{:}\)
  • \(1x\) and \(24x\)
    🔗
    🔗
  • \(2x\) and \(12x\)
    🔗
    🔗
  • \(3x\) and \(8x\)
    🔗
    🔗
  • \(4x\) and \(6x\)
    🔗
    🔗
Then, look through the list to find which pair adds up to \(11x\text{.}\) These are \(3x\) and \(8x\text{,}\) so we can fill in the area model figure as follows: We filled in the areas \(3x\) and \(8x\) first. Then the sides \(3\) and \(8\) were filled in. Thus \(x^2+11x+24= (x+3)(x+8)\text{.}\)
