Principle 2.2.33. Slope-intercept form.

If a line has slope \(m\) and has \(y\)-intercept \(b\text{,}\) then an equation for the line is
\begin{equation*} y = mx + b. \end{equation*}
To present a line in this format, substitute the known slope for \(m\) and the known \(y\)-intercept for \(b\text{.}\) In the final answer in slope-intercept form, do not substitue for \(x\) or \(y\text{,}\) which will remain variables.
